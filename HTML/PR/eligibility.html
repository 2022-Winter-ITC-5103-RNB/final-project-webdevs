Eligibility for Express Entry

- Express Entry class is for those workers who has work experience in canada and want to become permanent residents.

There are several areas where applicants need to meet requirements.

- Work Experience
- Education
- Language Ability
- Living Place

Work Experience

- Applicant should have at least 1 year of experience in canada in last 3 years before application

- There are different ways to meet this eligibility.

  1. Full-time job for one year which counted as 1560 hours.
  2. Part-time job for an equal amount of 1 year which counted as 1560 hours.
  3. Full-time job at multiple place.

- This experience should be obtained under the temporary resident status

→ Note: Unpaid internaship will not counted as a work experience.

According to the Canadian National Occupational Classification (NOC), skilled work experience can be classified in to three category,

  1. Managerial Jobs (NOC level 0)
  2. Professional Jobs (NOC level A)
  3. Technical jobs and skilled trades (NOC level B)
→ Note: To get max CRS point, applicant's job shoould be in NOC level A or NOC level B jobs.

Language Ability

- There is defined canadian language benchmark for different level NOC jobs.

- CLB 7 for NOC 0 or A jobs

- CLB 5 for NOC B jobs

English language assessment is based on four skills,

  1. Speaking
  2. Writing
  3. Reading
  4. Listening

- CELPIP(Canadian English Language Proficiency Index Program) and IELTS(International English Language Testing System) are the two known english language assessment organization and supported by IRCC department.

- Applicant must take CELPIP-General test and IELTS-General test for express-entry application.

- For french language, TEF and TCF are the two french language testing system approved by immigration officials for an assessment of french language skills based on speaking, writing, listening, reading.

- Result of language assesment are valid for 2 years and applicant can improve his CRS score by obtainig both english and french language exams.
