Process & Documents that Required to get Canada Student Visa:

If you’re planning to study in Canada as an international student, then you must have a study permit. Below are the steps to obtain a study visa.

1. Choose Canadian University/College

Before applying for the permit, the first thing you’ll need to do is choose which course you would like to choose and get an idea about college and university degrees. check out here about college vs university.

2. Apply to the University/College

After deciding on a program, we will help you prepare and submit your application. You need to have a letter of acceptance from a Canadian designated learning institution (DLI) before you can apply for a study permit.

3. Apply for the Study Permit

Once you have acceptance letter in your hand, you'll be ready to apply for your study permit.

Documents Checklist

  • Passport First & Last Page
  • All Academic Documents (Secondary & Highschool Marksheets, Bachelor All Marksheets, Transcript, Degree Certificate, etc.)
  • Internship Certificate (if Any)
  • IELTS result(if you're international student and previous studies in other language then English)
  • Parents Income Proof (2 Years IT Returns)
  • Any extracurricular activity or job details (Joining Letter, Appointment Letter, Experience Letter, Salary Slip) if available.
  • Medical Exam Copy
  • Statement of Pupose(SOP)
  • Passport Size photo

4. Pay Visa Processing and Biometric fees

Be mindful of the processing fees you’ll need to pay, this includes for yourself and anyone else on your application. You’ll also have to pay the biometrics fee. Keep in mind that you have to book your appointment for biometric nearest to your area or city.

Canadian Study Permit fees
Fee Amount in CAD
Restore status ($200) + new study permit ($150) $350
Biometrics – per person $85
Biometrics – Maximum fee for a family of 2 or more people applying at the same time and place $170

5. Wait for your result

The processing times can vary depending on the country you’re applying from. Applicants will generally have a waiting time of 16 weeks to get a Canada study permit, however, applicants from countries participating in the Student Direct Stream can expect shorter waiting times of approximately 20 calendar days.

How to Check your Visa Status?

- While applying for your study permit you have created your account on GCkey portal, using the same credentials you can login here.

- Upon application confirmation, you will receive the result of your biometric and medical exam on the portal. After passing all three exams, You will be examined by a visa officer and receive a PPR (Passport Request for Visa Stamp).
