Here are some common fall/winter clothing accessories to have:

  • For hands: Gloves or mittens
  • For neck: Scarf or neck warmers;
  • For feet:Socks that are made from wool or wool blend with silk or polypropylene;
  • For head and ears: Hat, toque, beanie, and/or ear muffs;

When and where should you shop for fall and winter clothing?

Many newcomers planning to arrive in Canada during fall or winter tend to go shopping for winter wear in their home countries. While it’s advisable to purchase some essentials to get through the initial days, you will find more variety and have access to clothing and accessories that are specifically built to withstand Canadian weather if you buy your winter gear in Canada.

This is especially true if you’re moving from temperate or tropical countries.

Every popular retailer and brand in Canada has sales at the beginning and conclusion of the seasons. Black Friday weekend in November and Canadian Thanksgiving weekend in October are also popular periods to discover amazing deals.

You can subscribe to newsletters and mailers from your favourite brands and merchants to remain informed about upcoming promotions.

Baby winter clothing for the outdoors

A bunting or snowsuit is a terrific way to keep your baby warm and protected from the elements. Most snowsuits have hoods and feature form-fitting legs and arms. Buntings are more like small sleeping bags, with a seam between the legs to help with stroller snap-ins.

You can use a baby carrier to keep your baby close to your body and avoid wrapping them in a snowsuit. Using jacket extenders, a baby carrier is inserted between your own jackets.

If you plan to take your baby out frequently throughout the winter, a fuzzy seat liner for the stroller or a stroller muff that provides extra bottom warmth may be worthwhile investments. When shopping for a stroller, seek for one with larger wheels for better all-terrain performance, as well as specially designed coverings to keep your kid safe from rain, snow, and wind.
