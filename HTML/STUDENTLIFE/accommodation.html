Accommodation

Begin by calculating your monthly budget.
In Canada, how much do students spend in rent per month?

When it comes to lodging, the first decision you'll have to make is how much you can afford to pay per month. The average rental price in Canada's larger cities (for example, Vancouver, Toronto, and Montreal) is greater than in smaller cities and villages.

In a shared accomodation setting, a fair starting point for rental cost is between $700 and $1,000 CAD per month per room (apartment, house, or student housing). Depending on where you reside, you might expect to pay about $1,200 CAD per month to live alone in a studio or one-bedroom apartment.

Utilities, transportation, food, and domestic furniture can all add up to a significant amount of money, so don't forget to factor them in.

Is it required for international students to live on campus?

If your programme does not provide housing, there is no requirement for overseas students to live on campus. However, depending on your university's policies, you may get preferential access to on-campus housing as an international student.
