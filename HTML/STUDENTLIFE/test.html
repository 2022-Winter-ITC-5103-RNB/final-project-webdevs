1. Representatives of Customer Service

Customer se rvice positions have risen to the top of the list as a result of the COVID-19 pandemic. In 2020 and early 2021, support lines received record calls from customers cancelling plans and asking refunds, or simply seeking answers to a company's COVID process. Customer support representatives are still in great demand, despite the fact that some of the confusion has subsided. Many businesses have closed or decreased their physical locations, or simply switched to entirely virtual operations. Furthermore, many call centre jobs are remote, allowing you to work from anywhere.

Ontario, Quebec, British Columbia, Nova Scotia, New Brunswick, Manitoba, Prince Edward Island, Alberta, Saskatchewan, and Newfoundland and Labrador are among the most in-demand regions.

IELTS Types:

IELTS is delivered in to types:

  1. Pen and paper based IELTS

  2. Computer Delivered IELTS

Both tests are same. The only difference is it only take three days to get the result of Computer Delivered IELTS whereas the result is declared after 13 days in Paper Based IELTS.

IELTS Module:

There are two modules of IELTS test:

Academic IELTS:

  • Measures whether your level of English language proficiency is suitable for an academic environment. It reflects aspects of academic language and evaluates whether youâ€™re ready to begin training or studying.

  • Intended for those who want to enroll in universities and other institutions of higher education.

  • For studying in Canada, international students have to secure good band score in Academic IELTS.

General Training IELTS:

  • Measures English language proficiency in a practical, everyday context. The tasks and tests reflect both workplace and social situations.

  • Intended for those planning to undertake non-academic training or to gain work experience, or for immigration purposes.

IELTS Parts:

IELTS consists of four parts:

  1. IELTS Listening : 40 mins
  2. IELTS Reading  : 60 mins
  3. IELTS Writing   : 60 mins
  4. IELTS Speaking : 11-14 mins

Total time for the test: 2 hours and 45 minutes

Listening, Reading and Writing are completed in one sitting. The Speaking test may be taken on the same day or up to seven days before or after the other tests.

IELTS Evaluation:

All four modules are scored individually and then average is calculated to generate overall band score.

The band score ranges from 0 to 9 with 0 indicating test taker did not attempt the test and 9 indicating expertise in the language.
